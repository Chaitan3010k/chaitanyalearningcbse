National Scholarship Portal Registration 2019-20 | Last Dates for Central, State and UGC Schemes

January 2, 2019 by Bhagya

National Scholarship Portal Registration: NSP is a digital scholarship program that offers various scholarships offered by the central government, state governments, and other government organizations like AICTE (All India Council for Technical Education), UGC (University Grants Commission), etc. This Portal hosts about 50 scholarships amongst the scholarship seekers worth hundreds of crores, posted on the platform. As per the administrators, NSP has supported the government obtains and serve scholarships of more than Rs.2000/- crores. The scheme challenges that they get 125 Lac applications each year, out of which 105 lakhs applications are the confirmed one.

National Scholarship Portal is a single platform by which many services beginning from candidate application, fee receipt, processing, approval and disbursal of several scholarships to Students are accepted. National Scholarships Portal is perceived as Mission Mode Project under the National e-Governance Plan (NeGP).

National Scholarship Portal Schemes

National Scholarship Portal comprises scholarships for students of all the classes such as standard 1st to Ph.D. level. There are most importantly three schemes provided by NSP under following categories:

  1. Central schemes
  2. UGC schemes
  3. State schemes

How to Register for National Scholarship Portal?

To register for NSP Scholarship Registration students need National Scholarship Application Form and they also need to follow the below steps;

  • Students registering for NSP scholarship for the first time (Fresh Students) have to “Register” on the National Scholarship portal as a fresh applicant by providing correct and authenticated details as mentioned on their documents in the “Student Registration Form”.
  • The application form is expected to be filled by parents/guardians of scholars who are below the age of 18 years at the time of registration.
  • Before starting the registration process, students /Parents/guardian are recommended to keep the following certificates handy:
    • Educational documents of the student.
    • Student’s Bank account number and IFSC code of the bank branch
      Note: For the pre-matric scholarship scheme, where students do not have their own bank account, parents can provide their own account details. However, parents’ account numbers can only be used against scholarship applications for a maximum of two children.
    • Aadhaar number of the Student
    • If Aadhaar is not available, then Bonafide student certificate from Institute / School and
    • Aadhaar Enrolment ID or Scanned copy of Bank passbook
    • If Institute/School is different from the domicile state of the applicant, then Bonafide student certificate from Institute / School.
  • Details to be filled in the application form are;
    • Date of Birth (DOB)
    • State of Domicile
    • Scholarship Category
    • Name of Student
    • Mobile Number
    • Email Id
    • Bank account details
    • Identification Details
  • After submitting the application, the default login id and password to log into the NSP portal will be sent to the provided mobile number. In case the password is not received, the option for forgot a password at the login page shall be used.
  • Students are advised to provide “Annual Family Income” as per the Income Certificate issued by a competent authority in their scholarship application.

In the case of Existing User

  • Apply for scholarship renewal with your ‘Application Id’ and Date of Birth which you registered previous year.
  • A student can also use ‘Forgot Application ID’ to retrieve their ID.
  • Only those students would be able to renew who had actually received the scholarship payments during the previous year from NSP.

Purpose of National Scholarship Portal

The Mission Mode Project (MMP) under the National e-Governance Plan of National Scholarships Portal tries to present a common computerized portal for executing many Scholarships schemes begun by Union Government, State Government and Union Territories across the nation. The main goals of NSP are;

  • Assured Scholarships have been given on time to the students
  • To present a common portal for several Scholarships plans of Central and State Governments
  • Build a clear database of students
  • Assure no duplication is made in processing.
  • The synchronization of various Scholarships schemes & standards
  • Application of Direct Benefit Transfer

National Portal Scholarship Benefits

  • All scholarship details are available under one platform and students can quickly apply through the sole integrated applications gateway for all the scholarships, presenting the process very easy for them.
  • The system of NSP will recommend the candidates the best plan for which they are qualified and will make assured there is no copy or duplication. This holds a sheer record.
  • Candidates can learn here at the All-India level, encouraging them to set the standard
  • Assists as a decision support system (DSS) for Ministries and departments since the latest data will be available on request.
  • Complete MIS System to improve monitoring every step of Scholarships distribution i.e. from registration for the scholarship to its delivery

FAQ’s on National Scholarship Portal

Question 1.
How do I register for the national scholarship portal?

Answer:

  • Log into the National Scholarship Portal (NSP)
  • Register Yourself
  • Logging into the National Scholarship Portal
  • Fill the National Scholarship Portal Application Form
  • Uploading the required Documents
  • Mention Contact Details

Question 2.
What is the last date of the National Scholarship Portal?

Answer:
The Institutes which are registered on NSP 2.0 are required to submit Institute Registration Form for AY 2019-20. The Form is available in Institute Login. The PMSS Scheme for WARB/Police Personnel (Martyr) will be opened from 6th November 2019.

Question 3.
Who are eligible to apply for Scholarship Schemes?

Answer:
Students fulfilling the Scheme guidelines of various Ministries are eligible to apply for these scholarships. These are available on the Home Page of the Portal.

Question 4.
How to submit the online application? Should I need the user id and password to apply for a scholarship?

Answer:
Applying procedures for Scholarship Schemes for both Fresh and Renewal are given below:

  • Fresh: Student has to Click on the option “Student Login”, on the home page of the National Scholarships Portal. Fill up the application as per the instructions are given by the system then click on the Save button. After saving, the student will get a “Temporary ID”. The system will instruct the applicant to submit his/her Temporary ID and date of birth to fill subsequent details. Once registration is complete on click of submit button, a Permanent Registration ID is generated which can be used for Renewal and tracking the status of the application
  • Renewal: Renewal Students have to apply with their Application Id and Date of Birth which they registered in the previous year. Students can also use Forgot Application ID to retrieve their ID.

Question 5.
How to track the status of my application?

Answer:
An option “Check Your Status” is available once you log in with Application Id and DOB.
